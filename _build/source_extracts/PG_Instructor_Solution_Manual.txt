P[Heading 1]: 5. Free Cash Flow Solution
P[Normal]: The assignment deliberately uses two FCF concepts. The textbook calculation starts with EBIT and operating NOWC. The cash-flow statement calculation starts with operating cash flow. P&G then reports an adjusted non-GAAP FCF that adds back the 2017 U.S. Tax Act payment.
P[Heading 2]: Why Textbook FCF and Cash FCF Differ
P[Normal]: The simplified NOWC definition captures accounts receivable, inventory, prepaids, accounts payable, and accrued liabilities. Operating cash flow also contains share-based compensation, deferred tax effects, gains or losses on asset sales, pension and other accruals, and additional operating timing items. The $822.23 million residual is therefore a reconciliation item rather than evidence that one of the two calculations is automatically wrong.
P[Heading 2]: Important Teaching Point
P[Normal]: Students must label P&G adjusted FCF as a non-GAAP measure and must not substitute it silently for textbook FCF. A good submission shows all three measures and explains their different definitions.
P[Normal]: Source: P&G FY2025 Form 10-K, report pp. 30 and 39.
P[Heading 1]: 6. Completed Free Cash Flow Waterfall
P[Normal]: Figure 2. P&G FY2025 FCF waterfall with textbook, cash-flow-statement, and company-adjusted totals.
P[Heading 2]: Required Sanity Checks
P[List Bullet]: NOPAT must equal EBIT plus the negative tax-on-EBIT bar.
P[List Bullet]: Textbook FCF must equal NOPAT + D&A - CapEx - ΔNOWC.
P[List Bullet]: Cash FCF must equal operating cash flow - capital expenditures.
P[List Bullet]: P&G adjusted FCF must equal cash FCF + the $562 million Tax Act payment add-back.
P[List Bullet]: The final reconciliation difference must be zero, subject to rounding.
P[Heading 2]: Common AI Errors
P[List Bullet]: Subtracting negative capital expenditures and thereby adding CapEx.
P[List Bullet]: Treating a negative operating NOWC balance as automatically bad rather than analyzing the change.
P[List Bullet]: Using the cash-flow statement working-capital effects as if they were identical to the balance-sheet change in the simplified NOWC formula.
P[List Bullet]: Calling P&G adjusted FCF GAAP free cash flow.

P[Heading 1]: 8. DuPont ROE Solution
P[Normal]: P&G's simplified consolidated ROE is high because a strong profit margin is magnified by a 2.408x equity multiplier. The relatively low 0.681x asset turnover is typical of a business with substantial goodwill, intangible assets, and manufacturing assets.
P[Normal]: Figure 4. P&G FY2025 simplified consolidated DuPont decomposition.

P[Heading 2]: Model Executive Interpretation
P[Normal]: P&G's FY2025 dashboard describes a mature, highly profitable consumer-products company. Net sales were nearly unchanged from FY2024, but operating income increased because SG&A declined and the prior-year Gillette impairment did not recur. Over FY2021-FY2025, net sales grew at approximately 2.6% annually while diluted EPS grew at approximately 4.3%, showing that per-share earnings outpaced top-line growth. Cash generation remained strong: cash FCF was $14.0 billion and P&G's adjusted FCF was $14.6 billion, although operating cash flow declined from FY2024. DuPont analysis shows that the approximately 31.2% simplified ROE reflects both a strong 19.1% profit margin and substantial financial leverage.
